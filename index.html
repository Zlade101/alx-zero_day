Nigerian Jollof Rice

A classic, smoky, and spicy West African rice dish. Jollof rice is a dish of immense cultural significance and a source of national pride for many West Africans and Nigerians are no exception with this sumptuous variant of Jollof. Let's get cooking!

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 10mins
Cooking Time: 40mins
Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes (or 1 can of chopped tomatoes)
  • 2 red bell peppers (Tatashe)
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (Atarodo), adjust to taste
  • 1 large onion, chopped (divided)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr) to taste

Cooking Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half of the chopped onion until smooth.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil. Fry the remaining half of the chopped onion until translucent.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and fry for 2-3 minutes to remove the sour taste.
  4. Pour in the blended pepper mixture and fry the sauce until it thickens and the oil begins to float to the top (this is your stew base).
  5. Stir in the stock, curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt, and seasoning cubes. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Wash the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear to remove excess starch.
  7. Add the washed rice to the boiling stew, stir until everything is well combined.
  8. Cover the pot tightly with aluminum foil before placing the lid on top. This traps steam and helps the rice cook evenly and develop a smoky flavor.
  9. Reduce the heat to low and allow the rice to steam and cook for 20-25 minutes without stirring.
  10. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving hot with your choice of protein (e.g., chicken, beef, fish).

Enjoy your Jollof!
